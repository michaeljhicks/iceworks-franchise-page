The Opportunity

Businesses Need Ice. They Don’t Want to Manage Ice Machines.

Ice is essential for restaurants, hotels, healthcare facilities, schools, offices, and service-based businesses. But owning and maintaining a commercial ice machine can be expensive, frustrating, and unpredictable. Iceworks solves that problem with all-inclusive leasing: equipment, installation, cleaning, maintenance, repairs, and support wrapped into one predictable monthly payment.

Essential Business Need

Restaurants, hotels, healthcare facilities, offices, and service-based businesses rely on ice every day. When the machine goes down, operations are disrupted.

High-Friction Ownership Problem

Commercial ice machines require cleaning, maintenance, repairs, filtration, and service coordination. Many businesses would rather pay one predictable monthly fee than manage the equipment themselves.

Recurring Lease Revenue

Multi-year customer agreements create predictable monthly revenue while giving customers reliable equipment, maintenance, and support without large upfront costs.

Why IceWorks

A Simple, Essential Business Built Around Recurring Revenue

Iceworks franchisees provide businesses with commercial ice machines through all-inclusive lease agreements that bundle equipment, installation, cleaning, maintenance, repairs, and support into one predictable monthly payment. It’s a practical, service-based model built around recurring revenue, long-term customer relationships, and an essential product businesses rely on every day.

Lower Startup Costs Than Many Franchises

No storefront, no large staff, and no retail inventory. Franchisees can build from a home-based operation while investing primarily in revenue-producing equipment.

Built for Lean Operations

With custom software, standardized documents, vendor relationships, and operational support, franchisees can manage customer relationships, billing, service coordination, and growth without a traditional brick-and-mortar footprint.

Proven Systems & Support

Iceworks provides training, launch assistance, marketing materials, lease documents, vendor relationships, software tools, and ongoing franchisor support to help franchisees build and manage their local market.

How It Works

FAQ

Common Questions

The initial franchise fee is $29,500. Total startup costs vary based on your market, launch plan, equipment purchases, installation costs, working capital, and financing structure. Because equipment is a major part of the business model, we walk candidates through the financial requirements during the application process.

No commercial ice machine experience is required. Iceworks provides training and support around the franchise model, customer relationships, leasing, installation coordination, service expectations, maintenance planning, and day-to-day operations.

Iceworks is designed to be a lean, home-based business without a storefront or large staff. However, franchise owners should expect to be actively involved, especially during launch and growth. Sales, customer relationships, financing, installation coordination, service follow-up, and local market development all require owner attention.

Each franchisee operates within an assigned territory defined by the franchise agreement. Territory structure is reviewed during the application process so candidates understand the market area, development expectations, and how Iceworks handles future growth.

Launch timing varies by candidate, market, financing, equipment availability, training schedule, and local sales activity. After signing, Iceworks helps new franchisees move through onboarding, training, launch planning, and the early stages of building their local machine portfolio.

Franchisees lease commercial ice machines to businesses through monthly agreements. The lease typically bundles the machine, installation, cleaning, maintenance, repairs, and support into one predictable monthly payment for the customer.

Franchisees own the ice machines. Franchisees purchase equipment from Iceworks Franchise Group, then lease those machines to local businesses through all-inclusive monthly agreements that include service, cleaning, maintenance, repairs, and support.

Many franchisees use financing to purchase equipment as they grow. The amount needed depends on machine count, equipment mix, installation costs, customer demand, and growth pace. Iceworks can introduce qualified candidates to lending partners familiar with the model.
